# Copywriting Frameworks Reference

Pick the framework that matches the offer's awareness level and price point.

---

## Awareness Levels (Eugene Schwartz) — pick before you write

1. **Unaware** — doesn't know they have the problem. Lead with story / pattern interrupt.
2. **Problem-aware** — knows the pain, doesn't know solutions exist. Lead with empathy then mechanism.
3. **Solution-aware** — knows solutions exist, comparing options. Lead with differentiation.
4. **Product-aware** — knows you exist, on the fence. Lead with proof + risk reversal.
5. **Most aware** — ready to buy, just needs the link. Lead with offer + price + CTA.

Mismatch is the #1 reason landing pages fail.

## Core frameworks

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
Best for: cold traffic, ads, top-of-funnel.
- **Attention**: pattern interrupt headline.
- **Interest**: relatable pain or surprising claim.
- **Desire**: vivid future-state imagery.
- **Action**: clear CTA, single click.

### PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solve
Best for: problem-aware audience, paid ads.
- **Problem**: name the specific pain.
- **Agitate**: amplify cost of inaction (time, money, embarrassment).
- **Solve**: introduce mechanism + proof.

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge
Best for: transformation offers (courses, coaching).
- **Before**: today's reality.
- **After**: the new reality post-purchase.
- **Bridge**: the offer is how you cross.

### FAB — Feature, Advantage, Benefit
Best for: bullet lists in value section.
- **Feature**: what the thing technically is.
- **Advantage**: what the feature lets you do.
- **Benefit**: why that matters in the user's life.

Example:
- *Feature*: "Private prompt vault, 200+ tested prompts."
- *Advantage*: "Skip prompt engineering for content tasks."
- *Benefit*: "Get back ~10 hours/week for client work that pays."

### 4Ps — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push
Compact, good for hero/sub combos.

## Headline formulas

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| `[Outcome] without [common pain]` | "Six-figure copywriting income without learning to code" |
| `How to [achieve X] in [timeframe]` | "How to build your first AI agent in 14 days" |
| `The [adjective] way to [outcome]` | "The lazy way to hit inbox zero" |
| `[Number] [things] every [persona] needs to [outcome]` | "5 prompts every founder uses to write better landing pages" |
| `Stop [bad behaviour]. Start [desirable behaviour].` | "Stop chasing tools. Start shipping revenue." |
| `[Aspirational identity]: a [thing] for [persona]` | "The Boardroom: a private circle for serious AI operators" |

Write 10 headlines. Throw 8 away. The 2 left are usually fine.

---

## Subheadline templates

- **Who-what-why**: "For [persona] who want [outcome] but are tired of [common bad option]."
- **Bridge**: "[Pain in one phrase] → [your mechanism] → [outcome]."
- **Identity**: "Built for [aspirational identity], not for [the masses they reject]."

---

## CTA verbs that beat "Submit"

`Get instant access` `Claim my seat` `Start my free trial` `Lock in my spot` `Send me the playbook`  
`Show me the templates` `Yes, I want in` `Reserve my access`

Avoid: `Submit`, `Click here`, `Learn more` (terminal — sends people to a wiki, not a checkout).

---

## Risk reversal phrases

- 30-day money-back guarantee
- "If [specific outcome] doesn't happen in [time], we refund the entire thing."
- Cancel-anytime, prorated to the day.
- "Your first week is on us — pay only if you stay."

---

## Price anchoring patterns

- **Price stack**: list every component with a $ value, sum it, then show actual price (e.g. "$3,200 of value, yours for $97/mo").
- **Comparison anchor**: "Less than your monthly Adobe bill."
- **Cost of inaction**: "One bad hire costs $50k. The Boardroom costs $97/mo."
